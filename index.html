My view on chemistry

Posted on 03-27-2016   |  

In the beginning of this semester, I set my self a goal: writing a blog article each month. March is coming to an end. I must finish this to keep my promise.
I’ve always want to write my opinion on chemistry periodically, so that when time pass by, I could look back and see what I was thinking on the road. Being a chemist have always been a dream of me since I was a little boy. The cartoon Tom and Jerry might be the initially propulsion: Jerry could be strong and powerful after drinking some magical liquid which is made in the flask. Meanwhile, I was deeply fascinated by the shining colors of chemicals (later on I find out not all chemicals are colorful). I like making stuff, this is probably given by the cartoon of Chinses medicine (I forgot which one) where pharmacists always boil herbs and make medicines. Now I think this interest is no longer limited in chemistry. It went wider and wider, machines, programs, music…as I know, there is more coming. Besides, I’m also really interested in those venomous animals. It is so amazing that such small amount of venom could actually kill people. I want to know what chemical is inside!
Now, I’m a Ph.D. student of chemistry, dealing with materials. In my view, the main tasks of a chemist are making stuff and understanding how make stuff. Though we are going through a time when the inter-discipline is becoming the tide of science, still the idea of chemistry is to develop. But this will not make chemistry merely a tool (though most of the time, it is), to understand how to make chemical makes chemistry scientific. Chemistry stand in the middle of physics and biology, the former one study the simplest things (in a way which is not simple) while the later one understands things as a dynamic complex.
We always say we synthesize. But what we are going to synthesize? A compound which is useful to human or something that is never existed on earth. I agree with what George S. Hammond said on this question.

“The most fundamental and lasting objective of synthesis is not production of new compounds, but production of properties.”
-George S. Hammond, Norris Award Lecture, 1968
As chemists, we should not merely think to mix chemicals together or adding some decoration of the existing system. Instead, we should think about what new properties we could create. From this point of view, organic chemistry and pharmaceutics are the same, even though they synthesize things in different scale. Thus, I prefer the saying “making a new property” to “develop something new”.
